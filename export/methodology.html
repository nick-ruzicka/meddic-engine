Step 1 · Discovery

From 7,115 ICP firms to 40 named accounts

We start with 7,115 ICP-qualified firms from SEC filings. The top 500 by AUM enter Tier 2 monitoring — firmographic scoring only, no contact enrichment. When a Tier 2 firm triggers a signal, it promotes to Tier 1 for full enrichment and contact scoring. The 40 Tier 1 firms are hand-curated top-tier institutions with named contacts, verified emails, and full account briefs.

Scoring Philosophy

MEDDIC as the scoring backbone

Every contact is scored against MEDDIC criteria. The four weighted dimensions below are not arbitrary — each one maps to a MEDDIC letter, so a composite score doubles as a read on deal qualification.
DimensionMEDDIC Mapping
ICP FitMetrics + Economic Buyer — is this firm the kind of account where the platform has proven ROI, and is the contact senior enough to influence a budgeted decision?
AI ReadinessIdentified Pain + Decision Criteria — does the firm have the workflow pain and an active evaluation framework, not just stated AI ambition?
ReachabilityChampion access — verified email to a named person plausibly positioned to convene the buying committee. A prerequisite for the rest of MEDDIC, not the Champion qualification itself.
Signal FreshnessDecision Process timing — recency of in-market behavior. Old signals decay; recent ones indicate the purchase window is open now.
Step 3 · Scoring

How we score each contact

A weighted composite across four dimensions. Every dimension is scored 0-100 by Claude given the firm, contact, and signal context.

THE THESIS

Institutional sales is won on depth, not cadence.

THE BUYER

This ICP rewards precision.

1

Senior buyers forward what's good. And what's bad.

  • These buyers talk to each other constantly. Peer networks are dense.
  • A great email gets passed around their peer group. A bad one does too.
  • The cost of getting it wrong is higher than anywhere else.
2

Evaluations happen around specific moments.

  • Nobody on this list is evaluating vendors continuously.
  • The buying signals that matter are discrete events — a new hire, a new mandate, a competitive deadline.
  • You catch those moments by paying attention before they happen.
3

The buyer has seen every pitch.

  • Every firm in this ICP is being pitched by dozens of AI companies right now.
  • Standing out isn't about being louder. It's about being the one that obviously did the work.
  • The goal is to make every first touch sound built for them — because it was.
